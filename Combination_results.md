Self + Care
You read the world through what is growing in you, and what kind of care allows it to continue.
This combination places your own position at the centre of a care-oriented sensitivity. Your instinct is to locate yourself inside a situation and ask whether that situation is being handled with enough attention, enough time, enough care.
This can show up as a natural tendency to audit your own handling of things. You are sensitive to the gap between what was given and what might have been needed. You notice when something wasn't tended to well enough, when more time was needed, when a situation deserved more care than it received. And because the lens starts from your own position, that noticing often turns toward your own contribution first.
This can make you attentive in ways others miss. You sense when something is being rushed, when a relationship needs more room, when a situation is asking for patience rather than action. You are often the person who stays with something long enough to understand what it actually needs.
What can throw you off is environments that move too fast for that internal process to happen, or situations where care isn't valued, or moments where you are expected to act before you have had enough time to tend to what you are noticing.
In everyday life this can look like asking whether you could have handled something better, needing time to process before responding, sensing when something important is being overlooked, and feeling the pull to repair or improve something before moving on.

Core line You read the world through what is growing in you, and what kind of care allows it to continue.
 
Social + Care
You read the world through the connections between people, and what those connections need in order to be sustained.
This combination places the relational structure at the centre of a care-oriented sensitivity. Your instinct is to read how people are connected and ask whether those connections are being given enough attention, enough time, enough care.
This can show up as a natural tendency to take stock of how a group or community is being held together. You are sensitive to the gap between what a shared structure is being given and what it actually requires. You notice when people have stopped showing up for each other, when bonds are being taken for granted, when a group is running on less than it needs. And because the lens starts from the whole structure, that noticing often turns toward what the group as a whole has or hasn't given.
This can make you attentive in ways others miss. You are often the person who senses what a community needs before anyone has thought to ask, who stays with a group dynamic long enough to understand what it actually requires.
What can throw you off is environments where the focus is purely on outcomes, where there isn't enough space for the health of the connections to matter, or where what you are sensing simply isn't being acted on.
In everyday life this can look like feeling the pull to tend to a shared dynamic before moving on, sensing when a group needs more time or attention, and asking not whether enough was done but whether enough was given.

Self + Order
You read the world through where you stand, and what in that space still needs to find its form.
This combination places your own position at the centre of an order-oriented sensitivity. Your instinct is to locate yourself inside a situation and ask what within it still lacks shape, what hasn't been named, what is sitting without a clear place.
This can show up as a natural tendency to bring form to your own experience. You are sensitive to the gap between what has happened and what has been acknowledged. You notice when your own position in a situation is still unclear, when something between you and a situation remains unrecognised, when something is sitting without a shape that makes it readable. And because the lens starts from your own position, that noticing often turns toward what you yourself have or haven't made clear. 
This can make you attentive in ways others miss. You are often the person who senses when something is still unformed, when the shape of a situation hasn't settled, before anyone else has felt it.
What can throw you off is environments where things are left deliberately vague, where naming something is treated as unnecessary, or where the form of a situation is never allowed to settle.
In everyday life this can look like sensing when something in a situation is still unformed, noticing when the shape of things is still unclear, feeling unsettled when something sits without a place, and staying with a situation until it has found a recognisable structure.
