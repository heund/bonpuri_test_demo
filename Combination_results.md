SELF + CARE
You tend to read the world through growth, timing, and personal becoming.
You notice when something is still forming. A project that needs more time. A person who is close to becoming something but has not fully arrived yet. A version of yourself that feels possible, but still needs the right conditions to take shape.
This means you often pay attention to what something is moving toward, not only what it looks like right now. You may sense potential before it becomes visible to other people. You may also feel frustrated when something is rushed, dismissed too early, or judged before it has had enough time to grow.
At the centre of this pattern is a strong awareness of becoming. You care about the conditions that allow a life, idea, relationship, or self to develop properly.

SELF + JUSTICE
You tend to read the world through origin, agency, and self-definition.
You notice when someone explains a person from the wrong starting point. A choice gets simplified. A strength gets misunderstood. A direction gets treated as luck, accident, or influence when it actually came from somewhere deeper.
This sensitivity often becomes strongest when the thing being misread is you. You may feel a strong need for your actions, intentions, and life direction to be understood from the right source. You may also recognise this pattern in others when someone gets blamed, underestimated, or explained too quickly.
At the centre of this pattern is a strong awareness of authorship. You care about where something truly begins, who gets to name it, and whether a person’s agency is being recognised accurately.

SOCIAL + CARE
You tend to read the world through connection, atmosphere, and shared support.
You notice the condition of the space between people. Someone going quiet. A relationship losing warmth. A group continuing as usual while something underneath has shifted. You often sense these changes before anyone says them out loud.
This sensitivity draws your attention to what keeps people emotionally held. You may notice who is carrying too much, who has been left alone with something, or where care has quietly stopped circulating. You may also feel pulled toward situations that need attention before they become harder to repair.
At the centre of this pattern is a strong awareness of relational maintenance. You care about what allows people, groups, and shared spaces to stay safe, responsive, and alive.

SOCIAL + JUSTICE
You tend to read the world through responsibility, fairness, and shared truth.
You notice how groups create a version of events. Who gets believed. Who gets blamed. What gets left out. What everyone agrees to move past because naming it would disturb the order of things.
This sensitivity often makes you alert to unresolved wrongs inside a group, family, institution, or community. You may remember what others have normalised. You may notice when responsibility has moved away from its real source, or when a situation has been made easier by leaving part of the truth untouched.
At the centre of this pattern is a strong awareness of collective accountability. You care about the gap between what actually happened and the version people have agreed to live with.
